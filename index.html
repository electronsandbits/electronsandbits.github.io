About Me

I recently graduated from UC Berkeley with my MS degree in Mechanical Engineering. As an undergrad, I double majored in Mechanical Engineering and Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS), also at UC Berkeley. Current Projects

Shared Perception for Connected Autonomous Vehicles V2V Communication Optimization

During high speed or high density driving, occluded vehicles present potentially dangerous obstacles that cannot be avoided until they are within the direct line-of-sight of our ego vehicle. In many situations, such as during unprotected left turns or lane merging, vehicles may be traveling at high speeds, making safe maneuvers difficult or even impossible. The goal for this project is to utilize Vehicle-to-Vehicle(V2V) communication and sensor fusion to generate a shared perception system between multiple vehicles in order to reduce occluded regions on the road and improve safety.

Past Projects

Convex MPC for Quadruped Walking Robot Matlab Optimization Robotics

Legged robots are complex, dynamical systems that are difficult to control due to their non-linear hybrid dynamics. However, there is extreme research interest in these types of robots because legged locomotion is far more efficient than wheeled locomotion when the ground terrain is uneven, unstable, or missing. In this project, we aim to simplify the quadruped dynamics so that it can be formulated as convex constraints, and use an model predictive controller to optimize for ground contact forces that will obey friction cone constraints while tracking a reference position and velocity.

Deep Reinforcement Learning for Mixed Integer Programs Python Optimization Deep Learning

Mixed integer programming is a heavily researched field that has applications in combinatorial optimization problems such as resource allocation and vehicle routing. However, the main drawback of combinatorial optimization is that exhaustive search of a large finite set can be computationally intractible. As a result, many state-of-the-art commercial solvers rely on hand-designed heuristics in their algorithms. These heuristics are typically designed by domain experts, making them work well for specific tasks but sensitive to changes in the problem statement. In this project, my group will try to use deep reinforcment learning techniques to automatically learn the best heuristics when solver mixed integer programs.

Robust Model Predictive Control for Multi-Train Dynamic Docking Matlab Optimization Modeling Advanced Controls

Dynamically docking multiple trains while ensuring safety has the potential to increase efficiency and reduce energy costs. Here we proposed multiple designs for dynamic docking including centralized and decentralized architecture, energy saving objective, and a robust model predictive control algorithm that safely docks multiple trains under uncertainties.

Hardware-in-the-loop simulation for RC car using virtual sensors Matlab Python Simulink

I participated in the Siemens FutureMakers Challenge with a group from Prof. Francesco Borrelli's Model Predictive Control lab. During this 24 hour hackathon, we developed a hardware-in-the-loop simulation using a 1/10th scale RC car and virtual environments and sensors using Prescan. The state of the vehicle is sent wirelessly to the computer simulator and corresponding sensor measurements and control inputs are sent back to the car in a loop. This project won first prize and resulted in a 6-month funded project for the PhD student in our group.

Fuel Saving Learning MPC Matlab Optimization Advanced Controls

The goal of this project is to reduce the fuel consumption of the ego vehicle over repeated runs of the same trajectory using a novel learning model predictive controller. The vehicle learns the slope of its path after each iteration, optimizing its force input in order to reduce fuel consumption while maintaining similar completion times.

Traffic Simulation with VisSim in PreScan PreScan VisSim Matlab Simulink

In this project, I modeled the road network in PreScan for a corridor located in Arcadia, CA. This corridor is outfitted with sensors that provide us with traffic flow and traffic density information at any given time. Using this real-world traffic data, I integrated the traffic flow and vehicle routing decisions using the popular microtraffic simulator, VisSim.
